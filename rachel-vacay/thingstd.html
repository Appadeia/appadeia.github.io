Things to do in Greenland

Dog Sledding

Tourists can go snowmobiling or skiing in Greenland, but nothing compares to the old tradition of traveling with dogs pulling your sled. It's an unforgettable experience of dogs joining forces to pull your sled. These adorable dogs love hard work and love to run in cold temperatures. They know the snow – sled dogs know where ice is too thin and don’t go there so it's safe.

National Park

Covering 972,000 km², this is the world’s largest park. It covers the entire north-eastern part of Greenland and is home sweet home for various Arctic species like seals and polar bears. For thousands of years, the Inuits have lived here thanks to these animals. Besides the impressive wildlife, visitors get to admire an incredible landscape, which features mountains, glaciers, fjords and icebergs.

Blue River

The Blue River is one of the most significant and gorgeous natural wonders of Greenland. It is a crystal clear turquoise river cuts a path through a frozen desert of ice. It's found on the Petermann Glacier, which feeds the river with its melting snow. Its shape changes every year due to this.

Cruising

Exploring Greenland by cruise is a fun time. Not only are sailors able to see the rocky and icy shoreline, but they also see various cities and settlements, since they are on the coast. Various cruises have different itineraries, letting you pick one depending on your preferences. Cute sea animals like seals and whales also are part of the unforgettable experience.
